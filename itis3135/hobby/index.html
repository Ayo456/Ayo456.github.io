What is Narrative Analysis to Me?

To me I think that Books, Shows, Movies, and Video Games all have a way of portraying a Narrative that is meant to resonate with its auidence. Books empower its narrative with the use of the reader's imagination to empower the effect of the narrative. Shows and Movies use actors, sets, and CGI to accurate display their own stories to the world. Video Games use the talent of voice actors, motion capture, musical scores, and programming to turn stories into interactive and immersive expereinces for players.

I think that it's important that we understand all of the work, talent, effort, and time that goes into making these creations for people. Understanding the narrative analysis behind these creations allows us to appreciate them on a deeper level and see the impact they have on our lives.

Who Brings These Stories to Life?

I believe that there are many different people and roles that are necessary to bring stories to life in various forms of media. I enjoy learning about the different aspects hat go into the creation of these stories so that I can appreciate them better. Authors are the creative minds that take their ideas and are able to turn them into stories that captivate readers. Directors are the visionaries who translate written narratives into visual spectacles, guiding the overall tone and pace of films and shows. On-screen actors embody characters, bringing them to life through their performances, while voice actors lend their voices to animated characters and video game personas, adding depth and emotion without physical presence.

Stan Lee is very well known for the creative issues of Marvel Comics which gained so much popularity that his work was adapted in the form of shows, movies, and video games. Andy Samberg is a well-known actor whos work in movies and shows (mostly comedies) have brought his characters to life in an eccentric way that resonates with the audience. Yuri Lowenthal is a well respected professional in the voice-acting field. His work in video games and animated shows has brought many characters to life through his vocal performances such as Sasuke Uchiha in "Naruto" and Spider-Man in various games.

When Do Stories Hit the Hardest?

For me a good time to enjoy stories is when I need to escape from reality for a bit. Whether it's after a long day at work or during a lazy weekend, immersing myself in a well-crafted narrative helps me relax and recharge.

Other times I like to are at night when I have some quiet time to myself. The stillness of the night, and even sometimes the ambience of snow or rain allows me to fully focus on the story without distractions, making the experience more immersive and impactful.

Where Can You Enjoy Stories?

Personally I like to enjoy immersing myself in stories in a place where I can comfortably sit for long periods of time. For movies and shows I like to sit in a comfy chair or couch with dim lighting to set the mood but sometimes my bed is just as good. When I play video games I like to be at my desk with my gaming setup so that I can be fully focused on the game without distractions.

Just as much as I can enjoy by myself, I also enjoy sharing these stories with friends and family. Whether it's watching a movie together or playing a co-op game, experiencing stories with others can enhance the experience and create lasting memories.

How Do Stories Work Beneath the Surface?

To me personally, stories hit the hardest in many ways. One of them is when you can personally connect with a character to where you can see yourself in them. Another way is when a story tackles real-world issues that resonate with the audience on a deeper level. Your own experiences can also shape how you perceive a story, making certain narratives more impactful during specific times in your life.

Beyond the obvious plot, stories work beneath the surface through recurring themes, character arcs, and motifs that shape meaning and emotional resonance. Subtext and symbolism communicate ideas without stating them outright, while point of view and voice control what the audience knows and feels. Careful pacing, foreshadowing, and the escalation of conflict build tension and investment, so the surface events reflect deeper truths and linger with the reader or viewer long after the story ends.

Why Do These Stories Matter So Much?

I think that aside from just creating media for entertainment, these forms of media also bring people together. Stories have the power to unite people from different backgrounds and cultures, and it can create a community of people who share similar interests and values.

They can also inspire change by shedding light on important social issues and encouraging critical thinking. Ultimately, stories matter because they reflect our shared humanity and help us make sense of the world around us. This is the reason that stories are important to our world, and we should openly encourage and embrace the art of storytelling.
